Hello Junior Web Designers.

I’m Chad and I will be your guide through ARTS 229 - Computer Graphics/Advanced Web Design.

I see this course as an exploration and expansion of all the stuff you did in ARTS 173. You should have a basic understanding of HTML and CSS. We will go much deeper with those, plus we will explore JavaScript and other coding languages. We will play with some of the different frameworks, systems, and libraries that are out there. You will create many web experiments and build several functional websites over the course of the class.

Web design is a constantly shifting landscape. Languages are evolving. Browsers frequently update with new capabilities. New techniques are shared on a daily basis. It is impossible to keep up with everything. I'm still not even sure what content is worth covering in this class. So, this class is really going to be a series of experiments and explorations. I will share ideas, resources, and techniques. You will also explore and share new ideas as well.

ASSIGNMENT/GRADING BREAKDOWN

  • Experiments - smaller assignments based on concepts and techniques discussed in class. You will follow in-class demos and modify to create your own variations. 30% of overall grade.
  • Explorations - self-directed explorations of code concepts and techniques. You will search for tools and techniques, build something, and share with the class. 20% of overall grade.
  • Projects - functional websites utilizing specific concepts from class. Basically, applying what you learn with the Experiments and Explorations. 40% of overall grade.
  • Participation - showing up to class, contributing to discussion forums, and participating in critiques. 10% of overall grade.
