The Wall Street Journal

I was hired by Saideh Ghods, founder of Mahak, to photograph a headshot of her that was published in The Wall Street Journal’s The 50 Women to Watch 2008 on November 7, 2008. This freelance opportunity introduced me to an inspirational person and to the intricacies of freelance work.

I was a part-time freelance photographer for several years and managed multiple projects in the US and abroad. I stopped freelancing in 2012 to make time for other professional goals.

Tools: Nikon D40, Velbon tripod, Adobe Creative Suite
